The Trial of The Dead Pope

In 897, Pope Stephen VI called the corpse of Pope Formosus for trial instead of letting the poor guy rest in peace. This bizarre history event has often been referred to as “one of the grisliest occasions in papal history.” Pope Formosus had a history of going against court rules. Pope Stephen VI conducted a church council, which became known as the “Cadaver Synod.” He dug out the body of his predecessor, who had died six years earlier, and put him on trial for perjury and violating church law. Pope Formosus was found guilty, stripped of his papal title, and his body was thrown into the Tiber River.
