The Death of Father Tragedy

Aeschylus, known as the father of tragedy in ancient Greece, met an extraordinary death. Tragedies from Greece and Rome often show characters facing death with an ironic twist of fate and this bizarre event is a testament to that. Legend has it that a tortoise fell from the sky after being dropped by an eagle. This eagle mistook Aeschylus’ bald head for a rock and dropped the tortoise intending to crack open its shell. Aeschylus’ unusual demise in 456 B.C. is remembered as one of the most ironic deaths in history, particularly fitting for the father of tragedy renowned for exploring human fate and irony.
